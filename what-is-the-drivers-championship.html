What is the drivers' championship?

The drivers’ championship is an official competition in motorsport for the best drivers in a given season. It is held in all forms of motorsport, from Formula One to stock car racing and everything in between. The drivers’ championship is the main competition in racing. It is the competition that determines who is the best driver in a given season. The drivers’ championship is awarded to the driver who has the most points at the end of the season. The number of points awarded for finishing first, second, third, etc., varies from one series to another. For example, in Formula One the points for finishing first, second and third are 30, 21 and 16 respectively. In NASCAR the points for finishing first, second and third are 25, 18 and 15 respectively. The number of points awarded for finishing first, second, third, etc., also varies depending on how many people are competing in a given series. The drivers’ championship is often referred to as the “driver’s championship” or “driver’s standings”, although it is actually a competition between the drivers themselves, not the cars.
